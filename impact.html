The impact

The results of the study

My research is about an aspect of the human condition, namely growth, and how this is experienced by undergraduates in the context of coaching. I argue that growth can be intentionally stimulated through coaching. I also explore what growth actually is for these students and how they experience it. This may help other students and professionals to recognise and encourage growth in the highly contextualised arena of university. I also offer recommendations for how you can help foster your own growth.

Student suicide rates and emotional distress levels could be reduced at university if we acted differently. We are obsessed by reactive policy once students hit the bottom of the waterfall; we need to be putting preventative policies in place to prevent them ever tipping over the edge.

Thus a coaching study is timely, in that coaching may well be one such preventative measure that can be put in place and offered to students, as a way to nip in the bud issues that could develop in mental health problems.
